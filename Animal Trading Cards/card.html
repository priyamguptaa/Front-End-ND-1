Tiger

Tigers overpower their prey from any angle, usually from ambush, and bite the neck, often breaking the prey's spinal column or windpipe, or severing the jugular vein or carotid artery.

Scientific name: Panthera tigris
Conservation status: Endangered (Population decreasing) Encyclopedia of Life
Trophic level: Carnivorous Encyclopedia of Life
Did you know: In captivity, adult tigers are fed 3 to 6 kg (6.6 to 13.2 lb) of meat a day.

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest cat species, most recognizable for their pattern of dark vertical stripes on reddish-orange fur with a lighter underside. The species is classified in the genus Panthera with the lion, leopard, jaguar, and snow leopard. Tigers are apex predators, primarily preying on ungulates such as deer and bovids.
